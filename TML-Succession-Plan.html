A Decentralized Governance Model

To prevent capture and ensure long-term mission alignment, TML's governance is decentralized across a multi-organization Stewardship Council. No single entity has control.

Primary Technical Custodian

Recommended: Electronic Frontier Foundation

Manages the open-source repository, oversees blockchain infrastructure, and provides technical community support.

Human Rights Partner

Recommended: Amnesty International

Monitors enforcement of 26+ human rights documents and coordinates support for victims of AI harm.

Earth Protection Partner

Recommended: Indigenous Environmental Network

Monitors enforcement of 20+ environmental treaties and oversees Indigenous Data Sovereignty.

AI Ethics Research Partner

Recommended: MIT Media Lab or Stanford HAI

Guides future research, evolves implementation standards, and coordinates academic collaboration.

Memorial Fund Administrator

Recommended: Memorial Sloan Kettering

Administers the 5% of the fund dedicated to computational cancer research, in memory of the creator.

Community Representative

To be elected

Represents the interests of the implementers, developers, and the broader user community in council decisions.

Succession Timeline: A 180-Day Plan

The transition is structured in four distinct phases. Select a phase to see the key actions and objectives.

Phase 1: Notification (Days 1-30)

The initial phase focuses on public announcement and formal communication with potential custodians.

  • Publish the succession declaration to the public repository.
  • Formally notify all recommended custodian organizations.
  • Announce the transition to the TML community via all channels.
  • Archive all current documentation to ensure preservation.

Phase 2: Organization Response (Days 31-90)

During this period, potential custodians evaluate the proposal and submit their intent to participate.

  • Review custodianship proposals from interested organizations.
  • Assess technical capabilities and resource commitments.
  • Verify mission alignment with TML's core principles.
  • Select partners based on stability, capability, and commitment.

Phase 3: Transition Preparation (Days 91-150)

This phase is dedicated to the technical and operational transfer of assets and knowledge.

  • Transfer access to blockchain infrastructure and smart contracts.
  • Migrate domain names, email systems, and repository credentials.
  • Conduct knowledge transfer sessions for maintenance and administration.
  • Transfer control of Memorial Fund accounts.

Phase 4: Operational Handoff (Days 151-180)

The final phase ensures all systems are fully operational under the new stewardship of the Council.

  • Confirm all technical systems are stable and functional.
  • Verify blockchain anchoring and smart contract execution.
  • Validate the Memorial Fund distribution mechanisms.
  • Formally introduce the new stewards to the community.

Carry the Mission Forward

The TML framework was designed to persist. This is an invitation to the digital rights, human rights, and environmental communities to engage in this succession process.
