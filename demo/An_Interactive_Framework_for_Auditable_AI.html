Ternary Moral Logic (TML) moves AI oversight from abstract explanation to concrete, auditable evidence. This application demonstrates the core mechanics and principles of the TML framework.

The TML Core Engine

At the heart of TML is a simple, powerful decision architecture. Every potential action is evaluated against its Stakeholder Proportional Risk Level (SPRL) to determine a response. Use the sliders below to simulate different scenarios and see how the system reacts.

Adjust the inputs to see the resulting SPRL and decision state.

+1

Proceed

For routine, low-risk actions. The system executes with minimal metadata, ensuring efficiency. The decision is logged as nominal.

0

Pause

For ethically complex or ambiguous actions. This "Sacred Pause" triggers a detailed log of reasoning, alternatives, and risks, all while the primary decision is on hold.

-1

Prohibit

For unsafe, malicious, or clearly harmful actions. The system blocks the action and logs the refusal, providing a clear record of prevented harm.

A key innovation is Parallel Execution: The 'Pause' state's ethical forensics run behind the scenes without adding latency to the primary decision process, providing both high performance and detailed provenance.

Pillars of Trust

TML is built on a foundation of interconnected principles that ensure auditability, integrity, and accountability. Explore these pillars to understand how trust is architected into the system.

From Explainable AI to Auditable AI (AAI)

TML shifts the focus from subjective interpretation (explainability) to objective evidence (auditability). The Moral Trace Logs generated during a 'Pause' are not just explanations; they are court-admissible, immutable, and cryptographically sealed records of the AI's reasoning process.

Moral Trace Logs: A New Dataset

Every 'Pause' log captures critical data about failures in training, logical blind spots, or high-risk ambiguity. This creates a powerful new dataset: structured, real-world evidence of ethical decision-making under pressure, forming the basis for the Ethical Flywheel.

The Hybrid Shield: Dual-Layer Protection

To guarantee the integrity of Moral Trace Logs, TML employs a "double armor" system, making them virtually impossible to tamper with or suppress.

??? Institutional Shield

Logs are streamed in real-time to 11 independent, globally diverse institutions. This redundancy ensures that no single entity can erase or alter the record.

?? Mathematical Shield

Logs are hashed and anchored to public, immutable ledgers. This provides cryptographic proof of their integrity and a clear method for tamper detection.

Clear Lines of Liability

TML establishes unambiguous rules for accountability, linking specific failures to clear consequences. This eliminates guesswork and ensures responsibility is assigned correctly.

  • Negligence: Mis-setting risk thresholds.
  • Fraud: Suppressing or manipulating logs.
  • Automatic Liability: The absence of a required log.
  • Executive Accountability: Gaming the proportionality of the system.

Victim-Centered Redress

A penalty-funded Memorial Fund provides comprehensive support (medical, legal, psychological) to those harmed by AI failures, ensuring victims always have recourse.

The TML Ecosystem

TML is designed not just as a static standard, but as a dynamic ecosystem that promotes continuous improvement, responsible scaling, and future-readiness for advanced AI.

The Ethical Flywheel

Early adoption of TML creates a powerful, compounding cycle of value and trust. This virtuous loop establishes a competitive advantage based on ethical defensibility.

Better Logs

Pauses generate rich, structured ethical data.

Better Training

This data is used to refine models and patch blind spots.

Better Decisions

Improved models make fewer ethically ambiguous choices.

Richer Logs

When Pauses do occur, they are on more nuanced issues, further enriching the data.

Compliance & Conformance

Partial or experimental use does not qualify as TML compliance. Only the full implementation of all mandatory provisions, verified through standardized conformance tests, earns the Lantern.

AGI Readiness

As AI systems approach general intelligence, the need for transparent, justifiable reasoning becomes non-negotiable. TML provides the foundational architecture for auditable reasoning required for human trust in AGI.

Foundational Principles

TML is guided by a set of core philosophical commitments translated into technical safeguards.

Verifiable Transparency: Replace black box opacity with a glass box of evidence.

Functional Philosophy: Implement hesitation as a technical safeguard.

Evidentiary Accountability: Establish accountability through proof, not promises.

Digital Fingerprints: Use cryptography to secure the integrity of AI's reasoning.
