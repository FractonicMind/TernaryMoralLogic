The TML Solution: A System of Enforceable Trust

TML embeds Earth's rights directly into AI's operational logic. It's not a suggestion; it's a computational requirement enforced by three core components working in unison.

The Rules Engine

Defines what cannot be broken: scientifically-backed Planetary Boundaries and culturally-vital Sacred Sites identified by Indigenous communities.

The Enforcement Mechanism

An immutable command, "Sacred Zero," that halts any AI operation threatening the rules. It is non-negotiable and absolute.

The Verification Layer

Sacred Sites & Indigenous Knowledge

Beyond global data, TML integrates hyperlocal, ancestral knowledge. Indigenous communities define sacred lands, waters, and ecosystems, which are given the same absolute protection as Planetary Boundaries, enforced through community-verified data.

● Community Sovereignty

Indigenous communities have autonomous control over defining and verifying their sacred sites within the network.

● Living Data

Knowledge is not static. Community observations of ecosystem health are fed into the Oracle Network as real-time data.

● Absolute Veto

A verified threat to a Sacred Site triggers Sacred Zero with the same authority as a planetary-level breach.
