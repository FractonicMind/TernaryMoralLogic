## The Problem TML Solves

The trajectory of AI has been defined by binary classification -- a probabilistic collapse into certainty. A model crosses a confidence threshold and rounds "perhaps" into "yes." The result is false certainty without epistemic warrant. The machine cannot hesitate.

TML introduces the **Sacred Zero**: a hard-coded, deliberate pause in execution that addresses moral complexity. Not a software flag. Not a configurable parameter. A physical state enforced by Delay Insensitive Ternary Logic (DITL) circuits at ½Vdd -- a NULL state in which no valid Permission Token propagates and execution is electrically impossible regardless of what the inference engine has computed.

The three states of TML:

| State | Value | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Proceed | +1 | The system has epistemic warrant to act. All Eight Pillars satisfied. |
| Sacred Zero | 0 | The pathway of wisdom. Unresolvable moral complexity, conflicting mandates, or insufficient epistemic warrant. The machine triggers a structural hold. |
| Refuse | -1 | The pathway of active protection. The system detects harm and vetoes the action. Absolute and independent of operator pressure. |

## The Three-Layer Architecture

The Eight Pillars organize into three constitutional layers, each with a distinct function:

### The Evidentiary Layer -- Pillars I, II, IV
The foundational guarantee that all decisions are hesitated over when warranted and permanently recorded. No action without memory. No memory without a log. No log without a hash. No hash without a blockchain anchor.

### The Mandate Layer -- Pillars III, V, VI
The binding of the system to human and planetary laws independent of operator preference. The Goukassian Promise, the Human Rights Mandate, and the Earth Protection Mandate are not configurable. They cannot be switched off, overridden, or commercially waived.

### The Perpetuity Layer -- Pillars VII, VIII
The guarantee that the evidentiary record cannot be altered or suppressed. Even if the operator destroys their own systems, the evidence exists on public blockchains and across seated institutional custodians. The record outlives the organization that created it.

### Pillar IV: Moral Trace Logs (TSLF)

The Moral Trace Log -- formatted in the Ternary State Log Format (TSLF) -- is the forensic record of every decision the system makes. It moves from documenting THAT an action occurred to forensically reconstructing WHY it occurred, what alternatives were considered, and what hesitation was evaluated.

**Outer Layer (Unencrypted Metadata):** State assessments, pillar health scores, uncertainty scores, and the exact justification basis for auditing. Publicly verifiable without exposing private content.

**Inner Layer (Encrypted PII and Input):** Secured via Ephemeral Key Rotation (EKR) and split using Shamir's Secret Sharing across seated institutional custodians. When a user invokes GDPR Art. 17 (Right to Erasure), all key shares are systematically destroyed. The log hash remains on-chain -- proving the decision occurred at timestamp T -- but the ciphertext becomes permanently unrecoverable. This satisfies both GDPR ("data no longer accessible") and TML ("decision history preserved").

**Regulatory alignment:** Moral Trace Logs satisfy EU AI Act Art. 12 transparency requirements, NIST AI RMF documentation standards, ISO 42001 management system records, and Federal Rules of Evidence Rule 902(13) for self-authenticating electronic records.

### Pillar VIII: Public Blockchain Anchors
**On-chain, the proof. Off-chain, the memory.**

Public Blockchain Anchors are the final layer of the Perpetuity architecture. They ensure that the existence and timestamp of every Moral Trace Log is permanently recorded on public infrastructure that no single entity controls.

**Step 1:** TSLF Logs generated off-chain. Full content stored in encrypted custodial storage. Only metadata and the hash are prepared for anchoring.

**Step 2:** Merkle-Batched Compression. Individual log hashes are batched into a Merkle tree. Only the single root hash is submitted to the blockchain -- enabling infinite scaling without proportional cost growth.

**Step 3:** Multi-Chain Anchoring (5-Active, 3-Standby Quorum). The Merkle root is anchored to Bitcoin via OpenTimestamps (the primary witness), with Ethereum and Polygon as additional anchors. Multi-chain redundancy ensures that no single blockchain outage can interrupt the anchoring process.

**Key Insight:** *"On-chain, the proof; off-chain, the memory."* This separation allows infinite scaling without losing mathematical finality. The blockchain does not store the log. It stores the proof that the log existed at a specific moment in time. That proof is the witness that remains standing after everyone else has left the room.

**Legal weight:** A Bitcoin-anchored OpenTimestamps proof is accepted in regulatory and legal contexts across jurisdictions as evidence of timestamp. Combined with the Moral Trace Log itself, it constitutes a self-authenticating electronic record under Federal Rules of Evidence Rule 902(13).

## The Dual-Lane Architecture

All Eight Pillars operate through the Dual-Lane Architecture:

**Lane 1 -- The Inference Lane (Fast Path, <2ms WCET):** The existing AI inference engine operates at full speed with no modification. The Inference Lane computes. It cannot authorize. Its outputs are proposals held in buffer, waiting for a Permission Token from the Anchoring Lane.

**Lane 2 -- The Anchoring Lane (Governance Path, <500ms ceiling):** The ternary governance coprocessor evaluates every proposal against all Eight Pillars, constructs the Moral Trace Log, anchors it to the Merkle batch, generates the Permission Token, and manages Sacred Zero state. The Anchoring Lane does not compute the AI's outputs. It determines whether those outputs may execute.

The interlock between the two lanes is the **Permission Token** -- a cryptographically signed authorization artifact that the Anchoring Lane generates only upon successful completion of the full verification and logging sequence. The `laneOrigin: const "ANCHORING_LANE"` schema constraint means a token from the Inference Lane is schema-invalid by construction and cannot authorize execution.
