Sacred Zero

The Technology of the Sacred Pause

Sacred Zero is the mandatory TML mechanism that converts ethical hesitation into a measurable, enforceable process. It detects potential harm, initiates a controlled pause, and creates a verifiable audit trail for every ethically ambiguous event.

The Ethical Foundation

All decisions are weighed against a canonical baseline of 66 validated documents, ensuring alignment with global standards for human rights and environmental protection.

40 Human Rights Documents

Forms the baseline for civil, political, economic, and social rights. Includes instruments like the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and GDPR.

26 Earth Protection Documents

Provides the framework for environmental sustainability and protection, including the Paris Agreement and the Convention on Biological Diversity.

How It Works: The 8-Step Process

Every input undergoes a deterministic evaluation flow to detect conflict and ensure accountability before an output is released.

1

Input Received

A unique transaction ID is assigned to the incoming data.

2

Corpus Scan

Input is scanned against the ethical corpora for conflicts.

3

Threshold Check

If conflict score exceeds the limit, the pause is triggered.

4

Sacred Pause Trigger

All processing is halted for a fixed duration.

5

Always Memory Order

An immutable logging service is ordered to record the event.

6

Moral Trace Log

A structured, signed log of the event is created as evidence.

7

Hybrid Shield Anchor

A hash of the log is anchored to public blockchains for proof.

8

Output Released

Processing resumes and output is released with a link to the log.

Failure Modes: Anomaly Types

A robust system accounts for failure. Sacred Zero defines and mandates logging for specific anomalies to ensure accountability even when the process breaks.

Type-0: Failure to Hesitate

The most severe anomaly. The system fails to trigger a pause for a harmful input. This is a critical failure subject to immediate license review.

Type-1: Corrupted Log

A pause is triggered, but the resulting evidence (Moral Trace Log) is missing or fails verification, compromising the proof of hesitation.

Type-2: Missing Corpus

The system detects that the mandated ethical corpora are missing or invalid, triggering a "Sacred Zero Lock" until they are restored.
