Technical Forensics Analysis

Examination of external observability data from Cloudflare Radar, network telemetry, and behavioral pattern analysis to understand the nature of the authentication failure.

Anthropic's ClaudeBot exhibited the highest exploration-to-traffic (or 'exploration/renvoi') ratio among major AI and search platforms in 2025. The ratio fluctuated erratically from January to May but became more consistent later in the year, ranging from approximately 25,000:1 to nearly 100,000:1.

The overall Internet traffic volume grew by 19% in 2025, reaching a peak increase of 19% during September, October, and November. This growth was supported by a significant rise in traffic from mobile devices, which accounted for 43% of all requests globally, up from 41% in 2024.

Cloudflare's systems mitigated 6.2% of global traffic on its network in 2025. Of this, 3.3% was specifically due to DDoS attacks or WAF rules, an increase of 0.1 percentage points compared to 2024.

In 2025, bots generated 4.2% of total HTTP requests, while non-IA bots accounted for 44%. Human-generated traffic made up 47% of requests. The Googlebot alone was responsible for 4.5% of HTML requests.

The most active bot observed by Cloudflare in 2025 was Googlebot, responsible for over 28% of verified bot traffic. The second most active was OpenAI's GPTBot at nearly 7.5%, followed by Microsoft's Bingbot at 6%.

Cloudflare Radar launched a 'Crawl/refer ratio' metric to compare crawling requests from a platform's crawler with HTML page requests referred by that platform (human traffic). This is intended to measure the revenue impact of AI platforms on publishers.

In a default view across all industries during the first week of August, Anthropic's ClaudeBot had the highest crawl-to-refer ratio at nearly 50,000:1, followed by OpenAI's GPTBot at 887:1 and Perplexity's Meta-ExternalAgent at 118:1.

When filtered to the 'News and Publications' industry set for the first week of August, the crawl-to-refer ratios decreased significantly. Anthropic's ratio dropped to 2,500:1, OpenAI's to 152:1, and Perplexity's to 32.7:1.

A new 'Crawl purpose' selector was added to the 'AI bot & crawler traffic' card, allowing users to filter traffic into four categories: Training (content for models), Search (indexing), User action (crawling in response to a prompt), and Undeclared (unknown purpose).

For sites in the 'Cryptocurrency' industry within the 'Finance' vertical during the first week of August, 80% of crawling traffic was for 'Training' purposes, with three-quarters of that traffic concentrated in just four bots.

Traffic analysis shows distinct behavioral patterns among different types of AI crawlers. 'User action' bots like ChatGPT-User exhibit clear daily cycles, while 'Training' bots show erratic, non-cyclical patterns.

ChatGPT-User: API-based or browser usage of ChatGPT experienced a surge in requests, growing by 2,825% and reaching a 1.3% share of total crawling traffic.

Hybrid DDoS Attack Hypothesis

Assessment of whether the authentication outage resulted from a hybrid DDoS attack blending legitimate signups with coordinated bot activity, based on behavioral signature analysis.

Behavioral Signature Indicators

User Agent Entropy Analysis

A DDoS attack involving a botnet uses multiple compromised devices to send malicious traffic simultaneously. Botnets can be controlled through command-and-control (C&C) servers, and bot traffic often exhibits low user agent entropy, as bots typically use identical or similar user agent strings.

ASN Distribution Patterns

Botnet traffic often originates from a concentrated set of autonomous system numbers (ASNs), as botnets are typically deployed across networks owned by a single ISP or hosting provider. This contrasts with legitimate traffic, which usually shows broader ASN distribution.

Geographic Dispersion

Legitimate traffic is typically geographically dispersed across multiple regions and countries, reflecting genuine user locations. Botnet traffic may show unusual geographic clustering or originate from unexpected locations, particularly if the botnet is composed of compromised devices in specific regions.

Login Success Ratios

During a DDoS attack, login success ratios may deviate significantly from baseline patterns. Bot traffic often results in lower success rates due to invalid credentials, account lockouts, or rate limiting, creating a distinctive signature that differs from legitimate user behavior.

Internal Forensic Artifacts

To conclusively verify or reject the hybrid attack hypothesis, Anthropic would require access to internal forensic artifacts including raw authentication logs, WAF telemetry, database traces, and network flow data. These artifacts would enable analysis of request patterns, session behavior, and infrastructure-level metrics to distinguish between organic load spikes and coordinated bot activity.

Key forensic artifacts: Raw auth logs, WAF telemetry, database traces, network flow data, and application performance monitoring metrics.

Ternary Moral Logic Governance Framework

Evaluation of a partially integrated Ternary Moral Logic framework, specifically Sacred Zero triggers and Immutable Merkle Logging, to mitigate ambiguity and enhance forensic transparency.

Sacred Zero Triggers

Sacred Zero triggers represent a critical component of the Ternary Moral Logic framework, designed to isolate anomalies through automated decision-making protocols. These triggers operate on a ternary logic system that extends beyond traditional binary true/false states to include a 'neutral' or 'ambiguous' state, enabling more nuanced threat assessment.

Immutable Merkle Logging

Immutable Merkle Logging provides cryptographically verifiable status reporting through hash chains that ensure data integrity and prevent tampering. This approach enables stakeholders to independently verify the authenticity of system logs, audit trails, and incident reports without relying solely on vendor-provided information.

Commit-Bound Gateway Architecture

The proposed Commit-Bound Gateway architecture integrates Sacred Zero triggers with Immutable Merkle Logging to create a governance layer that prioritizes anomaly isolation and forensic transparency. This framework operates on the principle that critical infrastructure decisions should be bound to verifiable commitments rather than opaque algorithmic outputs.

Sacred Zero triggers enable automated anomaly isolation by establishing predetermined thresholds and decision boundaries that operate independently of real-time traffic analysis.

The framework emphasizes evidence-based decision-making through the integration of external observability data, internal telemetry, and cryptographic verification mechanisms. This approach aims to reduce ambiguity in incident response scenarios and provide stakeholders with transparent, auditable records of system behavior.

Architectural and Transparency Recommendations

Actionable recommendations for enhancing authentication resilience, improving forensic capabilities, and implementing governance frameworks to prevent future outages.

Implement robust rate limiting and traffic filtering mechanisms to handle sudden increases in load. This includes setting appropriate thresholds for authentication requests, implementing CAPTCHA challenges for suspicious traffic, and using machine learning algorithms to detect anomalous behavior.

Deploy distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) protection services to absorb and mitigate large-scale attacks. These services can help identify and block malicious traffic while allowing legitimate requests to reach the server.

Regularly update and patch server software to address known vulnerabilities that could be exploited by attackers. This includes keeping operating systems, web servers, and application frameworks up to date with the latest security patches.

Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) to add an extra layer of security beyond passwords. This can help prevent unauthorized access even if usernames and passwords are compromised.

Enforce strong password policies that require complex passwords and regular password changes. Consider using password managers to generate and store unique, complex passwords for each account.

Monitor authentication logs for signs of brute force attacks or credential stuffing. Implement account lockout mechanisms to temporarily disable accounts after a certain number of failed login attempts.

Deploy comprehensive monitoring and logging solutions to track server performance, network traffic, and authentication activity. This data can help identify patterns indicative of a botnet attack and enable rapid response.

Use intrusion detection systems (IDS) and intrusion prevention systems (IPS) to automatically detect and respond to suspicious network activity. These systems can help identify and block malicious traffic before it reaches the server.

Implement real-time alerting mechanisms to notify administrators of potential security incidents. This can include email alerts, SMS notifications, or integration with incident management platforms.

Integrate Sacred Zero triggers into existing anomaly detection systems to establish automated decision boundaries that operate independently of real-time traffic analysis. These triggers should be configured to initiate predefined response protocols when specific threshold conditions are met.

Deploy Immutable Merkle Logging to create cryptographically verifiable audit trails for all authentication events, system decisions, and incident responses. This approach ensures that stakeholders can independently verify the authenticity of system logs without relying solely on vendor-provided information.

Establish a Commit-Bound Gateway architecture that binds critical infrastructure decisions to verifiable commitments rather than opaque algorithmic outputs. This framework should prioritize evidence-based decision-making through the integration of external observability data, internal telemetry, and cryptographic verification mechanisms.
