Clarifying Core Mechanics

A durable framework must withstand scrutiny. The following sections address common claims by clarifying the specific technical implementations of TML's pillars, demonstrating their robustness.

Claim: “Always Memory” conflicts with GDPR’s Right to Erasure.

Correction: TML integrates Ethical Key Revocation (EKR).

TML resolves the supposed paradox by design. EKR allows data to be rendered cryptographically unrecoverable, satisfying GDPR Article 17, without violating audit integrity. The evidence of the event remains verifiable via zero-knowledge proofs and hash continuity, but the link to personal identifiers is permanently broken by destroying the encryption key.

2. Encrypted Data (D) + Log Hash (H) Stored
↓
3. GDPR Request: Destroy Key (K)
↓
4. Result: (H) is verifiable, (D) is unrecoverable.

Claim: Public Blockchains cause energy conflict with Earth Protection.

Correction: Anchoring is modular and technology-neutral.

TML does not require direct, high-energy Proof-of-Work mining. The framework uses OpenTimestamps (OTS) and Merkle batching, which have a negligible energy impact. Public chains are optional verification layers, not operational dependencies. TML can use L2 chains or Certificate Transparency (CT) to achieve immutability without ecological conflict.

Claim: The “Hybrid Shield” is undefined and fragile.

Correction: The Shield is a formal dual-layer protection model.

The Hybrid Shield provides redundancy with no single point of failure. It is formally documented and consists of two independent, auditable layers that ensure the integrity and immutability of moral logs.

1. Mathematical Shield

Cryptographic anchoring of logs across multiple public ledgers for provable immutability.

2. Institutional Shield

Mirrored custody of logs across independent institutions (NGOs, universities) with rotating verification keys.

Claim: Human Rights codification is static and politicized.

Correction: TML uses a dynamic document-trigger mechanism.

TML does not hard-code treaties, which would embed interpretation. Instead, it references and hashes the latest canonical versions from official sources (e.g., UN, ICRC). This hash records the exact version and timestamp, preserving provenance and legal fidelity without politicized overrides.

1. Source: un.org/charter (Version 1.0)
↓
2. Log (T1): HASH(v1.0) + Timestamp
↓
3. Source: un.org/charter (Version 1.1)
↓
4. Log (T2): HASH(v1.1) + Timestamp

Claim: “Always Memory” implies perpetual personal data retention.

Correction: TML stores hashed, pseudonymized event proofs, not raw data.

This is a critical distinction. What persists is the *evidence of decision integrity*, not the personal content itself. This is the same principle used in forensics, where a file's hash is verifiable in court without revealing the file's substance. The permanence of Always Memory is evidentiary, not personal.
