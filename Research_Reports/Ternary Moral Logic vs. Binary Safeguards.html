A New Paradigm for AI Safety

This report explores Ternary Moral Logic (TML), a framework designed to provide a more nuanced and robust approach to AI safety by moving beyond simple binary decisions. Understand its core principles and how it compares to existing safeguard models.

✔

Permissible

Actions explicitly determined to be safe, ethical, and aligned with desired outcomes. The AI can proceed confidently.

✖

Impermissible

Actions explicitly determined to be harmful, unethical, or counter to safety constraints. The AI is forbidden to act.

?

Indeterminate

TML's key innovation. For novel, ambiguous, or ethically complex situations. The AI abstains and escalates for clarification.

Early Adopters of TML

TML is most likely to be adopted first in high-stakes industries where the cost of an incorrect autonomous decision is catastrophic. These sectors already prioritize "fail-safe" principles, making TML a natural fit. Click each card to learn more.

Autonomous Vehicles

In an unpredictable road environment, an AV might encounter a situation not covered in its training data. Instead of making a risky guess (e.g., misidentifying a person), TML would compel the vehicle to enter a safe state (e.g., slow down, stop) and alert the human driver, preventing potential accidents.

Medical Diagnosis AI

When an AI analyzes medical scans or patient data that is noisy, incomplete, or presents conflicting symptoms, a binary system might produce a low-confidence but potentially life-altering diagnosis. TML enables the system to officially report the case as 'Indeterminate' and flag it for immediate review by a human specialist, ensuring expert oversight on ambiguous cases.

Critical Infrastructure

For AI managing power grids or high-frequency trading, an anomalous event could trigger a disastrous cascade of decisions. TML provides a built-in circuit breaker. If an event doesn't match known safe or unsafe patterns, the AI can abstain from action, freeze the system state, and request human intervention, preventing widespread failures or financial losses.
