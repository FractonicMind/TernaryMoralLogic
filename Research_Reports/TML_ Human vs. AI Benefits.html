Who Benefits More from Ternary Moral Logic?

A forecast on the evolution of TML's impact on humans and AI over the next 10 and 100 years, exploring the path from control to symbiosis.

What is Ternary Moral Logic (TML)?

TML is an ethical framework that moves beyond a simple binary of "right" and "wrong." It introduces a third state, creating a more nuanced and flexible logic for machine decision-making. This is crucial for training AIs to navigate complex, real-world moral dilemmas.

1. Forbidden

Actions that are explicitly prohibited.

2. Permissible

Actions that are allowed but not required.

3. Obligatory

Actions that must be taken.

The 10-Year Horizon: A Tool for Human Control

In the near term, TML primarily serves to strengthen human oversight. It provides a clear, logical framework to govern autonomous systems, ensuring they operate predictably and safely within our existing legal and ethical structures. The focus is on accountability and control.

Over a century, TML evolves from a control mechanism into a shared ethical architecture. It becomes the foundation for a co-governance model where humans set principles and AIs execute them, creating a robust, self-regulating intelligent ecosystem.

Historical Parallels in Governance

The evolution of TML mirrors historical shifts where new forms of power were integrated into society through foundational rules and protocols.

Magna Carta

Established that even a monarch was subject to law, a principle TML applies to powerful AIs.

Constitutional Law

Created foundational rules for governance, much like TML creates rules for AI behavior.

Internet Protocols

A universal standard enabling complex systems to interact reliably, similar to TML's role for ethics.

Blockchain Consensus

A model for self-regulation within a network based on agreed-upon rules, a future state for TML-based AIs.

Conclusion: A New Arc for Civilization

Ultimately, the question may not be who benefits *more*, but how TML benefits civilization as a whole. In the short term, it preserves human control and ensures safety. In the long term, its greatest legacy may be far more profound: it doesn't just teach AIs how to follow our rules, it teaches intelligence itself how to be accountable.
