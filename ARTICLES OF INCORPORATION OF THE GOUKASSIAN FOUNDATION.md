## **ARTICLE III: PURPOSE**

### **Section 3.1 General Charitable Purpose**

This Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, scientific, educational, and literary purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code.

### **Section 3.2 Specific Purposes**

The specific and primary purposes for which the Corporation is formed are:

**(a) Public Safety Through AI Governance**  
 To advance the public safety, welfare, and ethical development of artificial intelligence systems through the research, development, maintenance, promulgation, and enforcement of the **Ternary Moral Logic (TML)** constitutional framework, a system of triadic decision logic designed to embed transparency, accountability, and moral reasoning into autonomous computational agents.

**(b) Standards Development and Maintenance**  
 To serve as the authoritative body for the TML technical specification, including:

* The maintenance of canonical documentation describing the Sacred Zero (State 0), Permit (State \+1), and Refuse (State \-1) logic states;  
* The ongoing revision and versioning of the TML specification through a transparent, community-driven governance process;  
* The publication of reference implementations, conformance test suites, and best practice guidelines.

**(c) Certification and Compliance Oversight**  
 To establish, administer, and enforce a voluntary certification program whereby organizations developing or deploying artificial intelligence systems may demonstrate adherence to TML principles through:

* Independent third-party audits;  
* Conformance testing against published standards;  
* Continuous monitoring and incident reporting;  
* The granting and, when necessary, revocation of official "TML-Compliant" certification marks.

**(d) Education and Training**  
 To provide education, training, and technical assistance to:

* Software engineers, AI researchers, and systems architects implementing TML-governed systems;  
* Policymakers, regulators, and legal professionals engaged in AI governance;  
* Corporate boards, compliance officers, and ethics committees responsible for high-risk AI deployments;  
* The general public regarding the societal implications of autonomous decision-making systems.

**(e) Research and Policy Development**  
 To conduct and sponsor research into:

* The ethical, legal, technical, and societal challenges posed by artificial intelligence;  
* The effectiveness of constitutional AI frameworks in preventing algorithmic harm;  
* The intersection of AI safety, human rights, environmental protection, and international law;  
* The development of policy recommendations for legislators and regulatory bodies worldwide.

**(f) Memorial and Archival Functions**  
 To preserve, honor, and perpetuate the intellectual legacy and ethical vision of **Lev Goukassian** (1971-2025), the creator of the Ternary Moral Logic framework, including:

* The maintenance of a digital archive of Goukassian's writings, correspondence, and technical contributions;  
* The establishment of the Goukassian Memorial Chair in Constitutional AI at leading academic institutions;  
* The curation of historical materials documenting the development and adoption of TML globally;  
* The administration of scholarships, fellowships, and awards in Goukassian's name to advance AI safety research.

**(g) Public Incident Database and Transparency**  
 To maintain a publicly accessible, blockchain-anchored database recording:

* All TML-certified systems (current and historical);  
* Aggregated, anonymized data regarding Sacred Zero events, refusals, and system performance;  
* Compliance audits, certification revocations, and enforcement actions;  
* Case studies and post-mortem analyses of AI incidents worldwide for research and educational purposes.

**(h) International Cooperation and Harmonization**  
 To collaborate with international organizations, governments, standards bodies, and civil society to:

* Harmonize TML specifications with emerging global AI regulations (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001);  
* Facilitate cross-border recognition of TML certifications;  
* Promote the adoption of constitutional AI principles in multilateral treaties and frameworks;  
* Serve as a technical advisor to the United Nations, OECD, IEEE, and other international bodies.

### **Section 3.3 Incidental Powers**

In furtherance of the foregoing purposes, the Corporation shall have all powers conferred upon nonprofit corporations under the Delaware General Corporation Law, including but not limited to the power to:

* Sue and be sued in its corporate name;  
* Acquire, hold, encumber, and dispose of real and personal property;  
* Make contracts and incur liabilities;  
* Borrow money and issue bonds, debentures, or other debt instruments;  
* Establish and maintain reserves and endowments;  
* Register and enforce intellectual property rights (trademarks, copyrights, patents);  
* Enter into collaborative agreements with other charitable, educational, and governmental organizations.

### **Section 3.4 Limitation on Activities**

Notwithstanding any other provision of these Articles:

**(a)** The Corporation shall not carry on any activities not permitted to be carried on by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code or by a corporation contributions to which are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Code.

**(b)** No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the Corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of its exempt purposes.

**(c)** No substantial part of the activities of the Corporation shall consist of carrying on propaganda or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, except as permitted under Section 501(h) of the Code (lobbying election), and the Corporation shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distributing of statements), any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.

**(d)** The Corporation shall not engage in any activity that would constitute an "excess benefit transaction" as defined in Section 4958 of the Code.

## **ARTICLE IV: MEMBERSHIP**

### **Section 4.1 No Voting Membership**

The Corporation shall have no voting members. All corporate powers shall be exercised by or under the authority of the Board of Trustees.

### **Section 4.2 Non-Voting Associate Membership (Optional)**

The Board of Trustees may, at its discretion, establish one or more classes of non-voting associate membership for:

* Organizations that have achieved TML certification;  
* Individual contributors to TML development;  
* Academic institutions and research centers;  
* Corporate sponsors and philanthropic supporters.

Associate members shall have no governance rights but may be granted:

* The right to attend annual meetings;  
* Access to members-only resources and technical documentation;  
* Recognition in official Foundation publications;  
* Participation in advisory committees (subject to Board approval).

## **ARTICLE V: BOARD OF TRUSTEES**

### **Section 5.1 Governance Authority**

The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by or under the direction of the Board of Trustees (the "Board").

### **Section 5.2 Number and Composition**

**(a) Size:** The Board shall consist of not fewer than seven (7) and not more than eleven (11) trustees, the exact number to be determined by resolution of the Board from time to time. The initial Board shall consist of nine (9) trustees.

**(b) Triadic Composition (Mandatory):** To operationalize the philosophical principles of Ternary Moral Logic, the Board shall maintain the following seat allocation:

| Category | Seats | Qualifications |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Technical Experts** | 3 | Advanced degree (PhD/MS) in Computer Science, AI/ML, Cryptography, or related field. Minimum 10 years professional experience in AI systems development. |
| **Ethicists** | 3 | Advanced degree (PhD/JD/ThD) in Philosophy, Law, Theology, Bioethics, or related field. Published work on technology ethics or AI governance. |
| **Stakeholder Representatives** | 3 | Experience representing civil society organizations, government regulatory bodies, or private sector AI deployments. Demonstrated commitment to public interest. |

**(c) Diversity Requirements:** The Board shall strive to ensure diversity of:

* Geographic representation (no more than 50% from a single country);  
* Gender and racial/ethnic background;  
* Professional affiliations (no more than 2 trustees from the same employer).

### **Section 5.3 Term of Office**

**(a)** Trustees shall serve for staggered three-year terms, with approximately one-third of the Board elected each year to ensure continuity.

**(b)** No trustee may serve more than two consecutive full terms (six years total), except that a trustee who has served two consecutive terms may be re-elected after a mandatory one-year absence from the Board.

**(c)** Notwithstanding the term limits above, the initial Board members shall be assigned staggered terms as follows:

* Three (3) trustees: one-year initial term;  
* Three (3) trustees: two-year initial term;  
* Three (3) trustees: three-year initial term.

The assignments shall be made by lot at the first Board meeting.

### **Section 5.4 Election and Appointment**

**(a) Initial Board:** The initial nine trustees shall be appointed by the Incorporator and named in the organizational resolutions adopted pursuant to these Articles.

**(b) Subsequent Elections:** After the first year, vacancies and expiring terms shall be filled by a vote of the remaining trustees. A two-thirds (2/3) supermajority vote of the full Board shall be required to elect or appoint a new trustee.

**(c) Nominating Committee:** The Board shall establish a Nominating Committee consisting of at least three (3) trustees, which shall:

* Solicit nominations from the TML community;  
* Conduct background checks and interviews;  
* Present qualified candidates to the full Board for approval.

### **Section 5.5 Removal**

A trustee may be removed from office, with or without cause, by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the remaining trustees at any meeting called for that purpose, provided that written notice of the meeting and the proposed removal is given to all trustees at least thirty (30) days in advance.

Grounds for removal with cause include, but are not limited to:

* Failure to attend three consecutive Board meetings without excuse;  
* Conviction of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude;  
* Breach of fiduciary duty or conflict of interest;  
* Actions materially harmful to the Corporation's mission or reputation.

### **Section 5.6 Resignation**

A trustee may resign at any time by delivering written notice to the President or Secretary of the Corporation. Such resignation shall take effect upon receipt unless a later date is specified, in which case it shall take effect on that date.

### **Section 5.7 Vacancies**

Vacancies on the Board, whether caused by death, resignation, removal, or increase in the number of trustees, shall be filled by the Board as soon as practicable. A trustee elected to fill a vacancy shall serve for the unexpired term of their predecessor.

### **Section 5.8 Compensation**

Trustees shall serve without compensation for their services as trustees. However, the Board may authorize reimbursement of reasonable expenses incurred in connection with their duties, including travel, lodging, and meeting attendance, subject to the Corporation's expense reimbursement policy.

### **Section 5.9 Meetings**

**(a) Regular Meetings:** The Board shall hold at least four (4) regular meetings per year, scheduled in advance. One such meeting shall be designated the "Annual Meeting" for the purpose of receiving reports and appointing officers.

**(b) Special Meetings:** Special meetings may be called by the President, any two trustees, or by written request of one-third of the Board.

**(c) Notice:** Written notice of all meetings shall be given to each trustee at least seven (7) days in advance, or forty-eight (48) hours in the case of emergency meetings. Notice may be delivered electronically (email, secure portal).

**(d) Quorum:** A majority of the then-serving trustees shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. If a quorum is not present, the meeting may be adjourned to a later date.

**(e) Voting:** Except as otherwise provided in these Articles or the Bylaws, decisions shall be made by a simple majority vote of trustees present at a meeting at which a quorum exists. Proxy voting is prohibited. Remote participation by videoconference is permitted.

### **Section 5.10 Committees**

The Board may, by resolution, establish one or more standing or ad hoc committees, each consisting of two or more trustees, to exercise such powers as the Board may delegate. All committee actions shall be reported to the full Board.

**Mandatory Committees:**

**(a) Audit and Finance Committee**  
 Responsibilities:

* Oversee annual independent financial audits;  
* Review and approve annual budgets;  
* Monitor financial controls and investment performance;  
* Ensure compliance with IRS Form 990 reporting requirements.

**(b) Governance and Nominating Committee**  
 Responsibilities:

* Manage trustee recruitment and nomination process;  
* Review and recommend updates to governance documents;  
* Conduct Board self-evaluations;  
* Oversee conflicts of interest policies.

**(c) Technical Standards Committee** (may include non-trustees)  
 Responsibilities:

* Maintain the canonical TML specification;  
* Review and approve TML Improvement Proposals (TIPs);  
* Develop conformance test suites;  
* Interface with the broader TML implementer community.

**(d) Compliance Oversight Panel** (may include non-trustees)  
 Responsibilities:

* Administer the TML certification program;  
* Conduct audits of certified systems;  
* Investigate complaints and whistleblower reports;  
* Recommend enforcement actions (certification revocation, litigation).

**(b) Vice President:** Assumes the President's duties in their absence; assists with Board initiatives.

**(c) Secretary:** Maintains corporate records; records minutes of Board meetings; manages official correspondence; ensures compliance with notice requirements.

**(d) Treasurer:** Oversees financial management; presents financial reports to the Board; works with auditors; ensures tax filings are completed.

**(e) Executive Director:** Hired by and reports to the Board; manages day-to-day operations; supervises staff; implements Board policies; prepares budgets and reports. The Executive Director may be compensated.

## **ARTICLE VIII: CONFLICTS OF INTEREST**

### **Section 8.1 Policy**

The Corporation shall adopt and maintain a Conflict of Interest Policy requiring:

**(a)** Annual disclosure by all trustees, officers, and key employees of:

* Financial interests in AI companies;  
* Consulting or advisory relationships;  
* Family relationships with persons having interests in the Corporation's activities;  
* Any other circumstances that could create actual, potential, or perceived conflicts.

**(b)** Recusal from voting on matters where a conflict exists;

**(c)** Independent review of compensation arrangements with related parties to ensure reasonableness;

**(d)** Documentation of all conflict-of-interest disclosures in corporate records.

### **Section 8.2 Rebuttable Presumption Procedures**

To satisfy Treasury Regulation ¬ß 53.4958-6(a), the Corporation shall follow "rebuttable presumption" procedures for establishing the reasonableness of compensation and transactions with interested persons:

**(a)** Transactions must be approved by an independent body (Board or committee) composed entirely of individuals without conflicts;

**(b)** The independent body must obtain and rely upon appropriate comparability data (salary surveys, market analyses);

**(c)** The independent body must adequately document the basis for its determination concurrently with making the decision.

## **ARTICLE IX: INDEMNIFICATION**

### **Section 9.1 Mandatory Indemnification**

The Corporation shall indemnify any trustee, officer, employee, or agent (each an "Indemnitee") who was or is a party or is threatened to be made a party to any threatened, pending, or completed action, suit, or proceeding (whether civil, criminal, administrative, or investigative) by reason of the fact that they are or were serving in such capacity, against expenses (including attorneys' fees), judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement actually and reasonably incurred in connection with such action, suit, or proceeding, to the fullest extent permitted by Delaware law.

### **Section 9.2 Advancement of Expenses**

Expenses incurred by an Indemnitee in defending any proceeding shall be paid by the Corporation in advance of the final disposition of such proceeding upon receipt of an undertaking by the Indemnitee to repay such amount if it is ultimately determined that they are not entitled to indemnification.

### **Section 9.3 Insurance**

The Corporation may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of any Indemnitee against any liability asserted against them and incurred in their capacity as a trustee, officer, employee, or agent, or arising out of their status as such, whether or not the Corporation would have the power to indemnify them against such liability under this Article.

### **Section 9.4 Limitations**

Indemnification shall not be provided to any Indemnitee:

**(a)** For acts or omissions involving intentional misconduct, fraud, or a knowing violation of law;

**(b)** For any transaction from which the Indemnitee derived an improper personal benefit;

**(c)** If indemnification is prohibited by applicable law or would jeopardize the Corporation's tax-exempt status.
